Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The client is DriverPass. The owner of DriverPass would like a system that helps student drivers prepare for their drivers test at the department of motor vehicles (DMV). The problem DriverPass would like to fix is students currently do not have enough preparation to be able to pass their drivers test at the department of motor vehicles (DMV). The client would like a system that solves this problem. 

What did you do particularly well?

I did a good job to determine functional requirements. 

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

I would revise the objectives and goals. I used a bullet list. I would describe this in complete sentences and keep my audience in mind. 

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I interpreted most of the user's needs well. I included their needs in the design. For example, the client wanted to be able to update packages. In my design, I included a class "Packages."

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I approach designing software with a methodical approach. From a high level, gather the user needs, create the requirements, design to meet all requirements, and test the system to ensure it meets the user's needs. I found the wide range of different charts to be useful in designing software. The UML activity diagrams are a great way to break down the use cases.
